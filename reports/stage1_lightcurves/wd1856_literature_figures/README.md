# WD 1856 Literature Figures

Local copies and PNG previews of selected figures from:

- Vanderburg et al. (2020), *A Giant Planet Candidate Transiting a White Dwarf*, Nature, DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-020-2713-y>, arXiv: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2009.07282>
- Limbach et al. (2025), *Thermal Emission and Confirmation of the Frigid White Dwarf Exoplanet WD 1856+534b*, ApJL in press at arXiv posting, arXiv: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.16982>

Use these in talks with on-slide credit to the original paper. The files here were extracted from the arXiv source bundles for internal presentation preparation.

## Quick Picks

1. `selected/vanderburg2020_extended_data_all_transits.png`
   - Best first discovery plot: TESS plus ground-based plus Spitzer transit observations.
2. `selected/vanderburg2020_fig1_gtc_spitzer_transits.png`
   - Best clean transit shape comparison: GTC optical and Spitzer infrared.
3. `selected/vanderburg2020_extended_data_archival_apertures.png`
   - Best background/contamination context: archival imaging, Pan-STARRS, and TESS aperture.
4. `selected/vanderburg2020_fig3_mass_age_constraints.png`
   - Best “planet vs brown dwarf” constraint from non-detection of thermal emission.
5. `selected/limbach2025_fig2_jwst_miri_sed_excess.png`
   - Best confirmation plot: JWST/MIRI mid-IR excess over the WD-only model.
6. `selected/limbach2025_fig3_temperature_fit_posterior.png`
   - Best physical confirmation plot: thermal-emission fit and temperature posterior.
7. `selected/limbach2025_fig4_temperature_radius_context.png`
   - Best context plot: WD 1856 b as a very cold directly detected exoplanet.
8. `selected/limbach2025_fig5_miri_false_color_image.png`
   - Best visual asset: JWST/MIRI false-color field image.
